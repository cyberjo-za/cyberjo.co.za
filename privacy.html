3. What Personal Information We Collect

We collect personal information that you voluntarily provide to us when you use our website, primarily through our embedded contact forms. The information we may collect includes:

  • Contact Information: Your name, email address, phone number, and any other information you provide in the message field.
  • Technical Information: We use Cloudflare to secure and optimize our website. Cloudflare may process minimal data, such as your IP address, as part of its security and analytics services. We do not use Google Analytics or other third-party tracking cookies for marketing purposes. Our website is built with static HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, which limits data collection to the essentials.

4. How We Use Your Personal Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • To respond to your inquiries and communicate with you.
  • To provide you with the services or information you have requested.
  • To maintain and improve the security and functionality of our website.
  • To comply with our legal and regulatory obligations.

5. How We Store and Secure Your Information

We take the security of your personal information very seriously. The information submitted through our forms is stored securely in a database that employs robust security measures, including:

  • Encryption: Sensitive personal information is encrypted at rest.
  • Access Control: Access to the database is protected by strong, unique passwords, and we enforce Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) where available.

6. Sharing and Transfer of Your Information

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information. We will only share your information in the following limited circumstances:

  • Cloud Service Providers: We may use third-party cloud services to store your information. When we do, we conduct due diligence to ensure the provider has security measures in place that are equivalent to or stronger than our own. We ensure that any transfer of data is secure and that the provider is bound by confidentiality agreements.
  • Legal Requirements: We may be required to disclose your information to comply with a legal obligation, court order, or request from a government authority.

In all cases, we take reasonable steps to ensure that your information remains secure and is handled in accordance with this policy.

7. Your Rights Under POPIA and PAIA

You have the right to manage your personal information. Your rights include:

  • Right to Access: You can request a copy of the personal information we hold about you.
  • Right to Correction: You can request that we correct any inaccurate or incomplete information.
  • Right to Deletion: You can request that we delete your personal information.

How to Make a Request:

To exercise your rights, please send an email to secure@cyberjo.co.za with the subject line "Personal Information Request."

Please note the following:

  • We will comply with deletion requests as best as we reasonably can. However, we may be unable to delete certain data, such as IP addresses contained within server logs, for security and operational reasons. In such cases, we will redact or anonymize the information where possible.
  • In accordance with the law, we may charge a minimal, reasonable fee to process your access or deletion request. We will inform you of any applicable fee before proceeding.

8. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or for other operational, legal, or regulatory reasons. The "Last Updated" date at the top of this policy will indicate when the latest changes were made. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.
